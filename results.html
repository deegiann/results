How Should You Choose Your Career?

If you've taken this assessment, you probably have some questions and doubts about your career path. You may be unhappy, but unable to pinpoint exactly why. Or, you may be considering a career change and feeling nervous about making the right choice.

Choosing a career can be an overwhelming decision, and it can be difficult to focus on what's important. Should you choose the career that will earn the most money? The one your friends or family have told you is best? Or should you focus on industries that are supposed to be "hot?"

The answer: none of the above. Choosing a career to please someone else, or to capitalize on buzzy trends, may seem like a safe way to go, but in the long run will only sap your time and energy. If you don't fit the career you've chosen, every day will be a struggle, and you'll wind up burned out and dreaming of an escape.

So what's the right way to choose a career? Think about who you really are. Appreciate the strengths and talents that you were gifted with. Acknowledge your weaknesses without shame. And then think about what's truly important to you in a career.
